My experiance in the Philippines

From 2015 - 2020 I was co-founder responsible for strategizing and implementing marketing & operation efforts of a solar transportation startup in the Philippines with head offices in Hong Kong.

Operations management

sunE had two shuttle operations in the Philippines, one of which ran 18 hours a day every day for 3 and a half years with more than half a million passengers served and 15 staff on payroll as well 6 electric vehicles in operation. I worked onsite and reported to the CEO in Hong Kong on a day to day basis and alongside the director of product development in the Philippines. The fleet consisted of 4 electric rickshaws and 2 electric Mitsubishi L300 vans which were converted from diesel. During that time I hired, trained and managed a growing team of shuttle drivers from lower income communities in the province of Batangas in the Philippines who could then enjoy a stable full time income and benefits. The shuttle operation ran without a single hour of downtime in over 1,000 days of operation

Brand

Designed the logo as well as the style guide for the brand. The idea of the sun with a solar cell merging with the sun gave a feeling of progress and motion as well as having three cells gave the original product sunEtrike the 3 wheeled tricycle. It also highlightlighted the 3 main tenets of clean, comfortable and cost effective. As the offer of sunE evolved, so did the brand. The challenge we had was that we were offering more than just a single vehicle but rather a full closed transportation system. This required me to develop collateral and graphics that we could be described and spoke to. Yellow and flat black became a challenge to design further assets with so blue was added as a secondary highlight colour to elicit a feeling of trust and familiarity while also a nice sky colour to complement the yellow in our branding.

Marketing efforts

As the director of marketing I organised logistics & marketing materials for multiple industry exhibitions and pitch competitions. THis was our most successful means of exposure as a small team as it allowed us to explain our evolving system in person to potential clients and partners. Through these events led to introductions to key contacts that led to two service contracts awarded to the company We were approached by Bertrand Piccard's Alliance of efficient solutions who were looking for 1000 labelled cleantech startups who addressed the planet's most pressing challenges. As Project manager of the application I amalgamated the business plan and technological documentation and brought them together into a successful application which led us to earn Solar Impulse Label. Being part of this network continues to bear fruit for sunE and had us registered to present at the IFC conference in Hong Kong in the startup category. Unfortunately the travel restriction implemented by coivd19 pandemic led to the event being cancelled.
